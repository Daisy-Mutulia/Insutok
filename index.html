The Problem

Why Content Creators
Fall Through the Cracks

Traditional insurance and social protection laws don't recognize content creators. When income stops, there's no safety net.

⚡

Algorithm Changes

Platform algorithms can demonetize or reduce reach overnight, wiping out months of income with zero recourse.

🏥

Illness & Health

Unlike salaried employees, content creators receive zero sick pay. A two-week illness can mean zero income.

🔗

Brand Deal Loss

Sponsorships can end abruptly due to company budget cuts, shifting trends, or contract disputes.

🚫

Account Deactivation

Platform bans eliminate all income instantly with limited appeals and no compensation.

📉

Audience Decline

Viral content fades. Income tied to views and subscriptions can halve in weeks without any wrongdoing.

⚖️

No Legal Protection

ILO, KIPPRA and Fairwork Kenya confirm: gig workers are excluded from social protection frameworks.

Actuarial Science

The Science Behind
Fair Pricing

Built on peer-reviewed actuarial models adapted for the realities of digital creator income in Kenya.

📊 Claim Frequency Model

Interruptions per year are modelled using a Poisson distribution — ideal for counting rare, independent events over time. Survey data from Kenyan creators shows an average of 2.0 interruptions per year, varying by platform and audience size.

📈 Claim Severity Model

Monetary loss per interruption is modelled using a Lognormal distribution — suited to right-skewed income with a zero lower bound. Each claim's financial impact is estimated using your income level, replacement ratio, and expected duration.

💰 Premium Calculation

Final premium combines expected loss with actuarial loadings for admin, profit margin, and adverse selection risk. Example: 2 interruptions/yr × KSh 15,000 severity × 1.20 loading = KSh 36,000/yr

🛡️ Risk Score Model

Risk is scored across three factors with actuarial weights, producing a composite score that determines your premium band. Scores range from 1.00 (highest risk) to 3.00 (lowest risk). High: 1.00–1.60 | Medium: 1.61–2.30 | Low: 2.31–3.00.

Risk Profile Explainer

How Your Risk Score
Is Calculated

📛

History of Bans — 50%

The most influential factor. Prior bans within 24 months signal a pattern of content-policy violations. Zero bans = score 1 (lowest risk). 3+ bans = score 5 (highest risk).

🎬

Content Niche Risk — 40%

High-risk niches (politics, controversial commentary, adult-adjacent) face greater algorithmic scrutiny. Low-risk niches (education, cooking, fitness) carry lower interruption probability.

📱

Platform Concentration — 10%

Relying on a single platform means a single point of failure. Creators on 3+ platforms have diversified income, making total interruption far less likely.

Risk Score Formula

Weighted raw score (1–5 scale) = (BanScore × 0.5) + (NicheScore × 0.4) + (PlatformScore × 0.1)

Mapped to final score: 3.00 − ((raw − 1) / 4 × 2) → range [1.00, 3.00]

Band classification: High Risk 1.00–1.60 · Medium Risk 1.61–2.30 · Low Risk 2.31–3.00

FAQ

Common Questions
Answered

Who is INSUTOK for?
INSUTOK is designed for Kenyan content creators who earn income from platforms like TikTok, YouTube, and Instagram. Whether you're a full-time creator or earn a portion of your income from digital content, INSUTOK can protect you.
What triggers a claim?
Covered events include platform account deactivation or bans, algorithm-driven demonetisation, illness preventing you from creating content, brand deal cancellations, and significant audience or income decline. Full terms in the policy document.
How is my premium calculated?
Your premium is based on three weighted risk factors: History of bans (50%), Content niche risk (40%), and Platform concentration (10%). These produce a risk score that feeds into an actuarial model to produce a fair, personalised premium.
How quickly are claims paid?
Straightforward claims verified through platform API data are typically approved within 48 hours of submission. Payments are made directly to your M-PESA or bank account. Complex claims may take up to 7 business days.
Is my personal and platform data safe?
All data is stored securely and never sold to third parties. The public data scan authorisation covers only publicly visible social media information for risk assessment. Documents you upload in claims are private and only accessible to the claims team.
